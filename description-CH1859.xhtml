Notes on Chapman and Hall First Edition (CH1859)
Copies examined:
CH1859-1[UVA] : PR5006 .O7 1859 Robert H. Taylor Collection
Three volumes in the publisher’s dull grey-green wavy-grained original cloth. Upper and lower covers blindstamped with identical pattern: central ornament and border (see photo of Heritage copy). Spine also blindstamped, but title, author, vol. no., and publisher name stamped in gold. Pale yellow endpapers, with large owner’s inscription in ink on the pastedown: Abb[ff?]etstoun[?]
Volume three contains 16 pages of advertisements at the back, dated July 1, 1859. Starts with “A LIST OF / NEW BOOKS AND NEW EDITIONS, / RECENTLY PUBLISHED BY / CHAPMAN AND HALL.”
 
CH1859-2[BH] : Private collection of Barbara Heritage, Charlottesville, VA
Book purchased in 2008 from the firm of Sumner & Stillman. Formerly owned by Sir Charles Henry Stuart Rich, Bart. (fourth baronet) and then Jean Hersholt.
Described by the firm as a “near-fine” example. Three volumes in the publisher’s dull grey-green wavy-grained original cloth (described by Sumner & Stillman as “greenish-brown”). Identical design to UVA copy (see photos of Heritage copy). Each volume contains the bookplate of Baronet Charles Henry Stuart Rich of Claxton Abbey on the front pastedown, and the signed bookplate of the Danish-born actor Jean Hersholt on the the back pastedown.
Volume three contains 32 pages of advertisements at the back, dated April, 1859. Starts with “A LIST OF / NEW BOOKS AND NEW EDITIONS / IN THE PRESS, / BY CHAPMAN AND HALL.”
Additional notes:
In George Meredith: A Bibliography, Michael Collie describes the 1859 Chapman and Hall first edition as containing “glazed primrose endpapers.” In addition to the “greenish-brown cloth” of the copies he saw (like the UVA and Heritage copies mentioned above), he also lists a variant binding of “reddish-brown cloth, with a blind-stamped variant decoration on the front and back covers and without the publisher’s name at the foot of the spine” as well as a copy in “grey cloth” noted by M. Buxton Forman in his bibliography (Collie 19).
Collie writes “some copies of the first edition of Volume III have 16 pages of advertisements at the end, dated 1 July 1859. One such copy is in the Berg” (19). The Heritage copy is the only one I know of to date that contains earlier ads.
--BH, 1 May 2008
